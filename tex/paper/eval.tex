\section{Implementation and Evaluation}\label{sec:eval}

The core of
\tool{}\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ciaran-matthew-dunne/pp2lp}}
is an OCaml program of about \toolOcamlLines{} lines behind a
Python command-line driver; the \lp rule library
is another \toolLpLines{}.
%
The pipeline has three stages.
%
A lexer and a Menhir-generated parser read the \replay{} file
into a flat list of (rule, argument, goal) records.
%
This list is then transformed into a proof tree, using each rule's
declared arity to attach the right number of child subproofs.
%
The two irregularities noted in \autoref{sec:prelim} are handled
at this stage: the \texttt{NRM}, \texttt{STOP\_NRM} and \texttt{FIN}
markers are filtered out, and the result derivation that precedes an
instance of \texttt{ALL7}/\texttt{XST8} is reassembled into that
rule's first argument.

%
\subsection{Benchmarks}\label{sec:bench}

We exercise \tool{} on four suites.
%
The \texttt{og} suite is \ogReplayed{} replays;
%
\texttt{prv} validates custom proof-rules from real \ab{} developments;
%
\texttt{claude} is a synthetic suite authored by an LLM agent (Claude
Opus~4.8) which we used to surface failures during development;
%
and \texttt{apero-mini} draws raw industrial proof-obligations from
CLEARSY's public dataset~\cite{deharbe2022pog},
using a custom translator from its POG format into \pp's \butf format.
%
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l r r r r}
\toprule
Suite           & Replays         & Verified    & Timeouts       & Failures \\
\midrule
\texttt{og}     & \ogReplayed     & \ogPass     & \ogTimeout     & \ogFailed \\
\texttt{prv}    & \prvReplayed    & \prvPass    & \prvTimeout    & \prvFailed \\
\texttt{claude} & \claudeReplayed & \claudePass & \claudeTimeout & \claudeFailed \\
\texttt{apero-mini}  & \aperoReplayed  & \aperoPass  & \aperoTimeout  & \aperoFailed \\
\midrule
total           & \allReplayed    & \allPass    & \allTimeout    & \allFailed \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
%
On the \texttt{og} and \texttt{prv} suites \tool{} reconstructs and
typechecks every replay.
%
In the \texttt{claude} suite, the \claudeFailed{} failures are
goals that PP succeeds in proving but we do not yet support.
%
\numRulesFired{} of the \numRulesCovered{} encoded rules fire across these
replays.
%
Re-emitting and re-checking these suites takes \benchWallAll{} on
\benchMachine{} (\benchCpuAll{} of \lp{} checking).

\texttt{apero-mini} restricts CLEARSY's corpus to obligations with fewer
than \aperoHypCap{} hypotheses --- by \ab{} folklore the regime in which
\pp{} performs best, and the band in which \textsc{replay} loses the fewest
of its traces.
%
Of the \aperoReplayed{} replays \tool{} reconstructs, \aperoPass{} type-check
within the budget and a further \aperoTimeout{} exceed it on the heaviest of
them; only \aperoFailed{} are genuine reconstruction failures.
%


\subsection{Conclusion and Future Work}\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented \tool, which checks \ab's Predicate Prover by reconstructing
its proofs in \lp.
%
Every inference rule of \pp{} is proved sound against a small trusted base,
and the reconstructed suites typecheck against that base, appealing to an
oracle for $\mathsf{INT}$ and $\mathsf{BOOL}$ memberships \pp{} assumes
(see below).

\paragraph{Upstream replay losses.}
%
Fewer replays reach \tool{} than the goals we submit
(e.g., only \prvReplayed{} of \prvGoals{} for \texttt{prv}),
and the loss is upstream: for some provable goals the replay utility
omits the subproof of an \rn{ALL7} or \rn{XST8} step,
and for others it aborts mid-file.
%
Both are detected at generation time and the affected goals dropped and reported.

\paragraph{Spec mismatch.}
The in-proof use of some rules does not match their specification:
\rn{NRM20} pins its equality at a different conjunct position than specified,
and \rn{OPR1}'s result derivation departs from its schema.
%
We implement the observed behaviour in \tool{};
a by-product useful to \pp's maintainers.
%
Also, the soundness of some rules for integer arithmetic and booleans
relies on set-membership hypotheses that the PP proof does not produce.
%
When such rules occur within proofs, we appeal to two axioms
$\mathsf{trust\_int}$ and $\mathsf{trust\_bool}$ to supply these conditions.
%
%

\paragraph{Use of AI.}
The \texttt{claude} suite was authored by an LLM agent which also helped
with some aspects of development, which introduces a risk of some of these
benchmarks not being representative of true use of \pp{}.
%
Many of the soundness proofs for PP's inference rules were also LLM-authored.
%
This risk is mitigated by \lp{}'s typechecking procedure,
and by observing that the agent did not insert any extra symbols
or rewrite rules outside of the proof scripts.

\paragraph{Future work.}
The most direct future work is upstream: repairing the replay tool's
truncations would recover the dropped goals, especially the equality-prover
fragment.
%
We also plan to develop a procedure to eliminate \pp{} specific symbols
from proofs (list-based conjunction, compound binders), which would allow
using \pp{} proofs in standard \lp formalisations.
%
Finally, folding \pp{} into the \dedukti{} ecosystem opens a natural ICSPA next
step: exporting checked \ab{} proofs to set-theory assistants such as TLA$^+$'s
TLAPS, Isabelle/ZF, and Megalodon.
