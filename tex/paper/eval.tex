\section{Implementation and Evaluation}\label{sec:eval}

\subsection{The tool}
\tool{}\footnote{\url{https://github.com/ciaran-matthew-dunne/pp2lp}} % TODO: confirm URL
is an OCaml program. An \texttt{ocamllex} lexer and a Menhir parser read a
replay; \texttt{rule\_db.ml} holds all rule metadata (arity, suffix
decoding, phantom predicate) and is the single source of truth;
\texttt{proof\_tree.ml} rebuilds the tree; and \texttt{translate.ml}
dispatches each rule shape to its emission. The rule encodings live in
\texttt{lp/rules/} and are checked once by \lp, independently of any proof.

\subsection{Coverage}
\tool{} encodes \numRulesCovered{} of \pp's roughly \numRulesSpec{}
specified rules. Only \numRulesNotImpl{} remain unimplemented---the
\rn{NRM27}--\rn{NRM30} family of arithmetic \emph{normalisation} rules,
which \pp{} discharges by dispatching to its internal arithmetic solver;
because the solver's reasoning is not recorded in the trace, these steps
have no checkable justification to reconstruct. A pre-commit audit
confirms that the only declared axiom is \texttt{B.lp}'s \(\trust\), and
that no emitted proof closes a goal with a bare \(\refine\,\trust\)
(\autoref{sec:trust}).

\subsection{Benchmarks}
We exercise \tool{} on four suites (\autoref{tab:results}). \texttt{og} is
a curated smoke-test floor; \texttt{synth} is a set of targeted
feature tests; and \texttt{prv} is \ab's own PRV proof corpus, with
\texttt{prv-no-arith} its non-arithmetic subset. Each entry is
\emph{emitted} by \tool{} and then \emph{checked} by \texttt{lambdapi
check}; ``pass'' means both succeed.

The non-arithmetic core reconstructs completely: \texttt{og}
(\ogPass/\ogTot) and \texttt{prv-no-arith} (\pvnaPass/\pvnaTot) pass in
full. On the complete \texttt{prv} corpus (\prvPass/\prvTot) the gap is
concentrated, as expected, in arithmetic: the unimplemented
normalisation rules above, together with proofs whose instantiation steps
use \pp's three-argument \rn{INST\_FINAL} form, which the parser does not
yet accept. Crucially, the failures we see are coverage and parsing gaps,
not soundness disagreements---no proof that \pp{} accepts and \tool{}
emits is \emph{rejected} by \lp{} on semantic grounds.

\subsection{Limitations}
The principal limitation is arithmetic normalisation that bottoms out in
\pp's closed-source solver (\rn{NRM27}--\rn{NRM30}); reconstructing it
would require either an arithmetic decision procedure on \(\El\,\iota\) or
a richer trace from \pp. Some set-theoretic surface syntax and the
three-piece \rn{INST\_FINAL} argument form are not yet parsed. These bound
coverage on \texttt{prv}; none affects the soundness of what is
reconstructed.
