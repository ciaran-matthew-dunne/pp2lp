\section{Encoding \pp{} in \lp}\label{sec:encoding}

\subsection{Encoding \pp's Predicates and Expressions}\label{sec:enc-formulas}

The encoding is shallow: \pp's predicates become \lp{} propositions and its
expressions \lp{} terms, and we assume the standard library's classical
axioms (excluded middle and propositional extensionality).
%
All B expressions inhabit a single domain, a set code $\iota$, so an
expression is a term of type $\El\,\iota$ and a predicate a term of type
$\Prop$.  The library's code $o : \Setu$, with $\El\,o ↪ \Prop$, lets a
predicate also stand where a `set' element is expected, which the
conjunction lists below rely on.

The kernel file \texttt{B.lp} fixes the trusted base.  Pairing $\mapsto$,
membership $\epsilon$, and B's application $\mathsf{eapp}$ are uninterpreted
constants over $\El\,\iota$, and the only axioms about them are the
injectivity and congruence of pairing and set extensionality.  Every
set-theoretic operator that survives \pp's pre-processing
(\autoref{sec:pp-syntax}) is likewise an uninterpreted constant.

Booleans and integers are not axiomatised from scratch but \emph{injected}
from the library types $\mathbb{B}$ and $\mathbb{Z}$, each by a map with a
left inverse:
\begin{align*}
  \mathsf{from\_bool} &: \mathbb{B} \to \El\,\iota, &
  \mathsf{to\_bool}\,(\mathsf{from\_bool}\,b) &↪ b, \\
  \mathsf{from\_int}  &: \mathbb{Z} \to \El\,\iota, &
  \mathsf{to\_int}\,(\mathsf{from\_int}\,z)   &↪ z.
\end{align*}
$\mathsf{BOOL}$ and $\mathsf{INT}$ name their images; $\mathsf{BTRUE}$,
$\mathsf{BFALSE}$, $\mathbf{0}$, and $\mathbf{1}$ are the injected library
values, and a numeral is an integer literal that \lp{} coerces into the
domain through $\mathsf{from\_int}$ automatically.

The arithmetic operators ($+$, unary $-$, and $\leq$) stay abstract over
$\El\,\iota$, so unification still recovers their operands, and take their
meaning from \emph{defining axioms} that a conservative orientation turns
into reductions of $\mathsf{to\_int}$:
\begin{align*}
  x + y    &= \mathsf{from\_int}(\mathsf{to\_int}\,x + \mathsf{to\_int}\,y), &
  \mathsf{to\_int}\,(x + y) &↪ \mathsf{to\_int}\,x + \mathsf{to\_int}\,y, \\
  {-x}     &= \mathsf{from\_int}(-\,\mathsf{to\_int}\,x), &
  \mathsf{to\_int}\,(-x)    &↪ -\,\mathsf{to\_int}\,x, \\
  x \leq y &= (\mathsf{to\_int}\,x \leq \mathsf{to\_int}\,y).
\end{align*}
Ground arithmetic therefore \emph{computes} in $\mathbb{Z}$ while the
operators stay rigid in goals; subtraction is sugar for $x + (-y)$, and
multiplication enters only as a literal coefficient.  \pp's arithmetic
rules, the laws of a discretely ordered abelian group, are then
\emph{proved} from these axioms (in \texttt{lemmas/Int.lp}), not postulated.
The trusted arithmetic base is thus $\mathsf{from\_int}$, its inverse
$\mathsf{to\_int}$, the defining axioms, and \lp's $\mathbb{Z}$; it is not a
bespoke construction of the integers (e.g.\ as equivalence classes of pairs
of naturals).

\paragraph{List-based Conjunction.}
\pp{} treats $\land$ as left-associative, so $p \land q \land r$ parses as
$(p \land q) \land r$; some rules peel this outermost conjunct, while others
reach a conjunct at an arbitrary position.  To serve both, we encode a
conjunction as a snoc list folded by one operator:
\begin{align*}
  &∎ : \mathbb{L}^{*}\,a, \qquad
   {∷} : \mathbb{L}^{*}\,a \to \El\,a \to \mathbb{L}^{*}\,a, \qquad
   \bwand : \mathbb{L}^{*}\,o \to \Prop, \\
  &\bwand(∎ ∷ p) ↪ p, \qquad \bwand(ps ∷ \top) ↪ \bwand\,ps.
\end{align*}
$\mathbb{L}^{*}\,a$ is the type of snoc lists over $\El\,a$ (empty $∎$, right
append $∷$), and the injective $\bwand$ folds a list of propositions
($a = o$) into their conjunction.  This conjunction stays \emph{abstract}:
its link to binary $\land$ is not a rewrite rule but a separate
axiomatisation, used only inside the rules' soundness proofs.  The list form
surfaces directly in a rule's type; \pp's \rn{AND2}, which refutes
$\bwand(ps ∷ p)$ by deriving $\lnot p$ from the rest, reads
\begin{center}
  \begin{prooftree}
    \hypo{H \seq \bwand ps \Rightarrow \lnot p}
    \infer1[\rn{AND2}]{H \seq \lnot\,\bwand (ps ∷ p)}
  \end{prooftree}
  \qquad
  $\rn{AND2} : \Prf\,(\bwand ps \Rightarrow \lnot p)
             \to \Prf\,(\lnot\,\bwand (ps ∷ p)).$
\end{center}

\paragraph{Compound Binders.}
\pp{} distinguishes the compound binder $\forall (x,y) \cdot P$ from the
nested $\forall x \cdot \forall y \cdot P$ and manipulates the former as a
unit, so we mirror it with a tuple type and quantifiers over it:
\begin{align*}
  \Tuple &: ℕ \to \TYPE, &
  \prj\,[n] &: ℕ \to \Tuple\,n \to \El\,\iota, \\
  \allI, \xstI &: (\Tuple\,n \to \Prop) \to \Prop, &
  \Dia, \Hrt &≔ \allI.
\end{align*}
$\Tuple\,n$ holds $n$ elements of $\El\,\iota$ with projection $\prj$;
$\allI$ and $\xstI$ are the $n$-ary universal and existential; and $\Dia,
\Hrt$ are aliases of $\allI$ that carry only a proof-search role
(\autoref{sec:pp-syntax}).  Each quantifier's introduction and elimination
are \emph{axiomatised} bridges to \lp's $\Pi$-types, e.g.\ for $\allI$
\[
  (\Pi v : \Tuple\,n,\ \Prf\,(P\,v)) \to \Prf\,(\allI v,\,P\,v)
  \qquad\text{and its converse.}
\]
They are axioms, not rewrite rules, by design: a rule unfolding $\allI$ to a
nested $\Pi$ would erase the quantifier from goals before a rule lemma could
match it, and recovering $P$ at each use would demand the higher-order
unification \lp{} does not attempt.

\subsection{Encoding Rules}\label{sec:enc-rules}
%
Each \pp{} rule becomes a \lp{} symbol whose type states the rule and whose
body \emph{proves} it sound.  Translating a rule's statement into that type
is positional:
\begin{align*}
  \text{a conclusion } H \seq P
    &\ \text{becomes the result type } \Prf\,P, \\
  \text{a premise } H, P \seq Q
    &\ \text{becomes an argument } \Prf\,P \to \Prf\,Q, \\
  \text{a side-condition (}P \text{ occurs in } H\text{)}
    &\ \text{becomes an argument } \Prf\,P,
\end{align*}
the last supplied by \tool{} at translation time (\autoref{sec:eval}).
Accordingly \rn{IMP4} (prove $P \Rightarrow Q$ by assuming $P$) takes its
premise as a function argument, while the discharge rule \rn{AXM1} (close
$P \Rightarrow Q$ once $\lnot P$ is known) takes its side-condition $\lnot P$
as a plain one:
\[
  \rn{IMP4} : (\Prf\,P \to \Prf\,Q) \to \Prf\,(P \Rightarrow Q)
  \qquad
  \rn{AXM1} : \Prf\,(\lnot P) \to \Prf\,(P \Rightarrow Q).
\]


\paragraph{Result Derivations.}
A result derivation (\autoref{sec:pp-syntax}) normalises a formula to a list
of predicates whose conjunction is its normal form.  Writing
$\mathsf{conj}\,ts$ for that conjunction ($\top$ when $ts$ is empty), we
encode the derivation as a type $\Res\,t$ pairing the list with a proof that
$t$ equals $\mathsf{conj}\,ts$:
\begin{align*}
  \Res &: \El\,o \to \TYPE \\
  \mathsf{mk\_res}\,[t : \El\,o]\,(ts : \mathbb{L}^{*}\,o) &: \Prf\,(t = \mathsf{conj}\,ts) \to \Res\,t \\
  \mathsf{res\_ts}\,(\mathsf{mk\_res}\,ts\,h) &↪ ts \\
  \mathsf{res\_eq}\,(\mathsf{mk\_res}\,ts\,h) &↪ h \\
  \restm\,r &≔ \mathsf{conj}\,(\mathsf{res\_ts}\,r)
\end{align*}
\noindent A result-form rule (suffixed \texttt{\_1} in replays) is a
\lp{} function over $\Res$ values.  \rn{AND4\_1} realises the combination
schema of \autoref{sec:pp-syntax}, concatenating its two children's lists;
\rn{ALL7}, one of the two rules that consume a result, takes a per-instance
derivation $\rho$ and continues under the conjunction ($\restm$) of its
result:
\begin{codeLPmulti}
\begin{lstlisting}[style=lp]
symbol AND4_1 [ps : $\mathbb{L}^{*}$ o] [p : Prop]
    (r1 : Res p) (r2 : Res ($\bigwedge$ ps)) : Res ($\bigwedge$ (ps ∷ p))
  ≔ mk_res (concat (res_ts r1) (res_ts r2)) (...);
\end{lstlisting}
\listsep
\begin{lstlisting}[style=lp]
opaque symbol ALL7 [n] [P : Tuple n → Prop] [Q : Prop]
    (ρ : Π v : Tuple n, Res (P v))
    : π ((`$\diamondsuit$ v, res_tm (ρ v)) ⇒ Q) → π ((`!! v, P v) ⇒ Q) ≔ ...
\end{lstlisting}
\end{codeLPmulti}
\noindent For example, consider the result derivation below, for the body
$P_1 \land P_2 \land P_3$ with each conjunct kept at a \rn{STOP\_1} leaf:
\begin{center}\footnotesize
\ebproofset{separation=1em}%
\begin{prooftree}
  \infer0[\rn{STOP\_1}]{(\seq P_3) \yields [P_3]}
  \infer0[\rn{STOP\_1}]{(\seq P_2) \yields [P_2]}
  \infer0[\rn{STOP\_1}]{(\seq P_1) \yields [P_1]}
  \infer2[\rn{AND4\_1}]{(\seq P_1 \land P_2) \yields [P_2, P_1]}
  \infer2[\rn{AND4\_1}]{(\seq P_1 \land P_2 \land P_3) \yields [P_3, P_2, P_1]}
  \hypo{}
  \ellipsis{}{\seq (\Dia x \cdot P_3 \land P_2 \land P_1) \Rightarrow G}
  \infer2[\rn{ALL7}]{\seq (\forall x \cdot P_1 \land P_2 \land P_3) \Rightarrow G}
\end{prooftree}
\end{center}
The example shows why results are lists, not conjunctions: the two nested
\rn{AND4\_1} steps concatenate their children's lists into the single flat
list $[P_3, P_2, P_1]$, which \rn{ALL7} conjoins.  Combining eagerly with
$\land$ would instead mirror the proof's nesting as $P_3 \land (P_2 \land
P_1)$; the list defers and flattens the conjunction.
%
Reconstructed, the \rn{ALL7} step becomes the proof term
{\small
\[
  \rn{ALL7}\ (\lambda v,\ \rn{AND4\_1}\ \rn{STOP\_1}\ (\rn{AND4\_1}\ \rn{STOP\_1}\ \rn{STOP\_1}))\ \ldots
\]}%
of type $\Prf\,((\allI v,\ P_1 \land P_2 \land P_3) \Rightarrow G)$, whose
continuation antecedent $\restm\,(\rho\,v)$ reduces to a plain conjunction
($\rho$ the derivation above, $v$ elided):
\begin{align*}
  \restm\,(\rho\,v)
    &≡ \mathsf{conj}\,(\mathsf{res\_ts}\,(\rho\,v))
     \;≡\; \mathsf{conj}\,(\mathsf{concat}\,(∎ ∷ P_3)\,(∎ ∷ P_2 ∷ P_1)) \\
    &≡ \mathsf{conj}\,(∎ ∷ P_3 ∷ P_2 ∷ P_1) \;≡\; P_3 \land P_2 \land P_1 .
\end{align*}


\paragraph{Arithmetic Side-conditions.}
A handful of rules carry an arithmetic side-condition that \pp{} discharges
with its own solver, leaving no subproof in the replay: \rn{AR4} needs
$(E+F) > \mathbf{0}$ for terms \pp{} believes positive, and the \rn{ARITH}
terminal closes a goal whose accumulated $\leq$-hypotheses are contradictory.
In the encoding each is an explicit $\Prf$-argument, so \tool{} must
\emph{generate} the missing proof at translation time.  A small
Fourier--Motzkin search over the in-scope hypotheses assembles the
certificate from the order lemmas of \autoref{sec:enc-formulas}, and any
linear $\mathbb{Z}$-equality it needs along the way (a reassociated sum, a
scaled coefficient) is closed by \emph{proof by reflection}, reifying each
side and comparing normal forms; we take this reflexive procedure from
Coltellacci and Merz~\cite{coltellacci2025lia} and refer there for its
construction.

For \rn{ARITH} the search looks for a nonnegative
(Farkas-style~\cite{schrijver1986}) combination of the $\leq \mathbf{0}$
hypotheses that sums to a false literal.  For instance, from
$h_1 : \Prf\,(x \leq \mathbf{0})$ and
$h_2 : \Prf\,(\mathbf{1} - (x + x) \leq \mathbf{0})$ the combination
$h_2 + 2h_1$ yields $\mathbf{1} - (x + x) + x + x \leq \mathbf{0}$, and
rewriting along the generated equality
$e : \Prf\,(\mathbf{1} = (\mathbf{1} - (x + x)) + x + x)$ leaves
$\mathbf{1} \leq \mathbf{0}$:
\[
  \mathsf{one\_not\_leq\_zero}\,\bigl(\mathsf{leq\_subst\_l}\ e\
    (\mathsf{add\_leq\_zero}\ \_\ \_\ (\mathsf{add\_leq\_zero}\ \_\ \_\ h_2\ h_1)\ h_1)\bigr)
  : \Prf\,\bot .
\]
Here $e$ is itself closed by reflection, so \pp's sum reorderings need no
bespoke permutation lemmas.


\paragraph{Soundness of the rules.}
Every rule symbol carries a checked body, not a postulate: the inference
rules are \lpmod{opaque} proofs and their result forms transparent
definitions over $\Res$, and either way \lp{} accepts the symbol only once
that body type-checks against the axiomatic base.  The proofs follow the
structure of the encoding: propositional and quantifier rules discharge to
the classical axioms and the quantifier bridges, conjunction rules to the
$\bwand$ eliminators, equality rules to congruence and substitution, and
arithmetic rules to the integer laws of \autoref{sec:enc-formulas} (and,
through them, \lp's $\mathbb{Z}$).  What remains trusted is therefore exactly
\texttt{B.lp}'s primitives: the pairing and extensionality axioms, the
boolean and integer injections with their defining axioms, the quantifier
bridges, and the $\bwand$/$\Res$ interface.
